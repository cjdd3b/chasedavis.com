Chase Davis

I run the Interactive News desk at The New York Times, a team of developer/journalists that uses code to solve problems in news. We work on major events like the 2016 elections and Rio Olympics; collaborate regularly on stories and interactives; develop new story forms, coverage models and products; and build tools to support newsgathering. We're always experimenting.

I also teach advanced data journalism at Mizzou and co-founded a consultancy, Hot Type Consulting, which is known for helping build and launch the Texas Tribune. Once upon a time, I worked as a reporter and editor at the Center for Investigative Reporting, The Des Moines Register and the Houston Chronicle.
